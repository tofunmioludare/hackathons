The Problem

AI is making legal decisions.
Nobody can prove what it decided.

Today, AI agents issue benefits decisions, flag contracts for rejection, and inform judicial recommendations — and when those decisions are challenged, the input, reasoning, and output have typically vanished. No court-ready record. No way to prove what the model saw, what it concluded, or whether a human ever reviewed it.

⚖️
Unattributable Decisions
AI agent decisions today are anonymous. The reasoning disappears. The input is not retained. Appeals have no evidential foundation and regulators cannot verify compliance retroactively.
Legal Risk
🏛️
Regulatory Exposure
EU AI Act enforcement begins August 2026. Legal AI is explicitly high-risk under Annex III. Penalties reach €35M or 7% of global revenue. Most firms have no compliant audit infrastructure today.
Compliance Gap
🔍
No Independent Verification
Internal logs are mutable. Database records can be edited. Without a tamper-proof audit trail, no AI legal decision can be trusted by courts, regulators, or opposing counsel.

Who We Are

Built by people who care
about the humans on the other side

APEX AUDIT started from a simple observation: the people most affected by AI decisions in legal and public services are rarely the ones with the power to question them. A benefits claimant whose application is rejected by an algorithm has no way to know what the system saw, what it concluded, or whether any human ever reviewed it.

We find that deeply unacceptable. And we believe distributed ledger technology — specifically Hedera's Consensus Service — is the first credible technical answer to it. Not because it is fashionable. Because it is genuinely tamper-proof in a way that no internal database can claim to be.

Every message on an HCS topic is immutably sequenced and timestamped by network consensus. A court, a regulator, or a claimant's solicitor can query that topic independently, without trusting any party. That is what court-ready evidence looks like in a world of AI agents.

We are building APEX AUDIT to be the compliance layer that every legal AI deployment will need — not as bureaucratic overhead, but as a genuine guarantee that the humans at the end of these decisions have a record they can use.

"The question is not whether AI will make legal decisions at scale. It will. The question is whether there will be a tamper-proof record of every one of those decisions that any party can verify — independently, without asking anyone's permission."
— APEX AUDIT · Core Principle
